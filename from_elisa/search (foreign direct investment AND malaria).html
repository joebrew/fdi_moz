    Title:

    Public Governance, Health and Foreign Direct Investment in Sub-Saharan Africa


    Author: Azemar, Celine; Desbordes, Rodolphe
    Author Affiliation: U Glasgow; U Strathclyde
    Source: Journal of African Economies, August 2009, v. 18, iss. 4, pp. 667-709
    Publication Date: August 2009
    Abstract: Using 1985-2004 yearly panel data for 70 developing countries, including 28 from Sub-Saharan Africa (SSA), the paper finds that once market size is accounted for, SSA's foreign direct investment (FDI) deficit with other regions of the world is mainly explained by the insufficient provision of public goods: relatively low human capital accumulation, in terms of education and health in SSA. On the basis of additional cross-sectional data, the paper finds that in the absence of HIV and malaria, net FDI inflows in the median SSA country could have been one-third higher during 2000-2004, with slightly more than one-half of this deficit explained by malaria.
